Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve

  • The sight of a tiger, the snarl of a leopard, high speed movement of the crocodile and the strategising wild dogs will always keep the memories of Tadoba fresh in the memory.
  • Crocodiles in the central lake are just as interesting to observe, as the leopards and tigers are.
  • Drive into the Park in an open Gypsy vehicle and just wait by the lakeside or at Panchadhara or Pandharpauni or just anywhere on the route where you can hear the symphony of birds.

Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve

Tadoba is probably one of India’s finest Tiger Reserves. The success of protection and conservation mechanism has meant the number of Tigers in this reserve have increased close to the optimal holding capacity.

This has meant that the herbivore population has been maintained in balance and thus the flora too is able to grow and regenerate continuously. In other words, the Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve boasts of a healthy forest.

Interestingly, new monitoring technologies available have also shown a steady movement and an exchange of population of animals like the tiger with other neighbouring forests – some even in the neighbouring states. This has meant a genetically healthy population of animals too.
